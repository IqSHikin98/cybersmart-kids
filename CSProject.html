Age-Specific Learning Modules (Infographics)

Primary Learners (Ages 7-12) ▼

Your Digital Safety Toolkit

Follow these three steps to keep your games and accounts safe like a superhero's base. You can also read the full story in the **Storybook** tab!

The Secret Key

Keep your passwords a secret. Use a mix of words and numbers that only you know.

Warning Pop-Ups

If something promises free prizes or looks tricky, use the 'X' to close it immediately.

Always Tell a Grown-Up

If you feel sad, scared, or confused online, stop and talk to a parent or teacher right away.

Teenagers & Social Media (Ages 13-17) ▼

Managing Your Digital Footprint

Protect your accounts and reputation with these three essential online defenses.

Activate 2FA (Double Lock)

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for all social media and email accounts. It's vital.

Review Privacy Settings

Check who can see your location and posts. Default to "Friends Only" to limit your digital footprint.

Block, Report, Tell

If you experience or witness cyberbullying, screenshot it, block the user, and report it to the platform and an adult.

University & Professionals (Ages 18+) ▼

Enterprise-Level Resilience & Threats

Four critical strategies for protecting highly sensitive institutional and personal data.

Verify & Scrutinize

Check email headers, look for domain mismatches, and verify sensitive requests via phone call.

3-2-1 Backup Rule

3 copies of data, 2 different media, 1 copy off-site. Your best defense against data loss.

Use a VPN Shield

Never access sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi without using a VPN to encrypt your connection.

Critical Patch Management

Treat all software updates as security fixes. Enable auto-updates to close backdoors attackers exploit.

Slides: Quick Cybersecurity Concepts

These slide-style sections offer condensed, bulleted information suitable for quick review or presentation preparation.

Slide 1: Anatomy of a Cyber Attack

  • **Reconnaissance:** Attacker gathers information (e.g., targets using LinkedIn or social media).
  • **Weaponization:** Creating the tool (e.g., malicious attachment, phishing website).
  • **Delivery:** Sending the weapon (e.g., email, compromised USB drive).
  • **Exploitation:** Executing the code on the victim's device.
  • **Installation:** Installing malware or backdoors for persistence.
  • **Actions on Objective:** Achieving the goal (e.g., stealing data, encrypting files).

Slide 2: Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA, 2010) - Fundamentals

  • **Purpose:** Regulates the processing of personal data in commercial transactions in Malaysia.
  • **Seven Principles:** Outlines how data must be managed (e.g., General Principle, Notice and Choice Principle).
  • **Your Right:** Individuals have the right to access and correct their personal data.
  • **Enforcement:** Monitored by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner.

Interactive Flashcards: Test Your Vocabulary

Click the card to flip between the term (Front) and the definition (Back). Perfect for memorizing key concepts.

PHISHING

A fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information (like usernames, passwords, and credit card details) by disguising as a trustworthy entity in an electronic communication.

RANSOMWARE

Malware that encrypts a victim's files, demanding a ransom payment to restore access to the data.

VPN

Virtual Private Network. Encrypts your internet connection, creating a secure tunnel for data transfer, especially useful on public Wi-Fi.
